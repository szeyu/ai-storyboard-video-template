description: Onboarding Interview Workflow - Use this workflow before ideation when the project brief is empty, vague, or missing decisions needed to start a storyboard video project.
---

## Goal

Interview the user just enough to create a useful brief. Do not over-interview.

Save answers into:

- `00-brief/project-brief.md`
- `00-brief/constraints.md`
- `00-brief/onboarding-notes.md`

## Interview Flow

Ask questions in small batches. Start with the essentials:

1. What are we making?
2. Who is the audience?
3. What is the main message or offer?
4. Where will it be posted?
5. How long should it be?
6. What style should it feel like?
7. What must be shown?
8. What must be avoided?

Then ask only the follow-up questions needed for the project:

- Product or brand details
- Required scenes, people, locations, packaging, or props
- Visual references in `04-assets/`
- Tone: premium, funny, UGC, cinematic, educational, corporate, etc.
- Format: 9:16, 1:1, 16:9, or custom
- Language and caption needs
- Deadline or number of variations
- Tool preference, such as Higgsfield or another generator

## Output

After the interview, write:

1. A concise brief in `00-brief/project-brief.md`.
2. Constraints in `00-brief/constraints.md`.
3. Raw assumptions, open questions, and user notes in `00-brief/onboarding-notes.md`.

Then continue with `.agents/workflows/main-video-workflow.md`.

## Rules

- Ask the fewest questions needed to start.
- If the user gives a rough idea, turn it into a structured brief.
- Mark assumptions clearly.
- Do not generate ideas or storyboard until the brief has enough direction.
- If source files are needed, ask the user to place them in `04-assets/`.
